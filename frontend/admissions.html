Free Residential Education for Tribal Students

EMRS Dornala provides completely free education, accommodation, meals, uniforms, and other facilities to selected tribal students from Class VI to XII. Admissions are granted through a national-level entrance examination conducted by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India.

Eligibility Criteria

Requirements for admission to EMRS Dornala

For Class VI Admission

  • Must belong to Scheduled Tribe (ST) category
  • Should have passed Class V from any recognized school
  • Age between 10-12 years as on 31st March of admission year
  • Valid ST certificate issued by competent authority
  • Medical fitness certificate

For Class IX Admission

  • Must belong to Scheduled Tribe (ST) category
  • Should have passed Class VIII from any recognized school
  • Age between 13-15 years as on 31st March of admission year
  • Valid ST certificate issued by competent authority
  • Medical fitness certificate

Documents Required

  • ST Certificate (original and photocopy)
  • Birth Certificate
  • Previous year's mark sheet
  • Aadhaar Card
  • BPL Certificate (if applicable)
  • Passport size photographs (4 copies)
  • Medical fitness certificate
  • Transfer Certificate (after selection)

EMRS Entrance Examination

Details about the selection test

Exam Pattern

The EMRS Entrance Test is a written examination conducted at various centers across the country. The test evaluates students' aptitude in key areas:

For Class VI

  • Mental Ability - 50 marks
  • Language Competency (English/Hindi/Telugu) - 30 marks
  • Mathematics - 30 marks
  • Science - 20 marks
  • Social Studies - 20 marks
  • Total: 150 marks
  • Duration: 2.5 hours

For Class IX

  • Mental Ability - 50 marks
  • Language Competency (English/Hindi/Telugu) - 40 marks
  • Mathematics - 30 marks
  • Science - 30 marks
  • Social Studies - 30 marks
  • Total: 180 marks
  • Duration: 3 hours

Exam Centers

The entrance test is conducted at various centers across Andhra Pradesh and neighboring states. Candidates can choose their preferred center while filling the application form.

Syllabus

The syllabus for the entrance test is based on the NCERT curriculum of the previous class (Class V for Class VI admission, Class VIII for Class IX admission).
